However, this strategy is not always successful, as mergers can be costly and may not always result in the development of new drugs. It is important for the government to consider policies that encourage innovation and the development of new drugs, rather than relying solely on mergers and acquisitions to sustain the industry.

Why crashing lithium prices will not make electric cars cheaper

20 April 2023

The price of lithium, a key component in electric vehicle batteries, has been on a rollercoaster ride in recent years. After a twelve-fold increase in prices due to the surge in EV sales, prices have now halved due to slowing demand in China and carmakers reviewing their supply deals. However, the global supply of mined lithium is rising fast, and prices are unlikely to fall far enough to bury big miners' profits. In the longer run, rising demand for lithium energy storage, supported by green policies in America, Europe, and China, could make the market even tighter.

Opinion: The recent fluctuations in lithium prices highlight the importance of diversifying the sources of critical minerals for EV batteries. The US and other countries should invest in domestic mining and recycling of lithium and other minerals to reduce their dependence on foreign sources. The US government should also consider policies to promote the development and adoption of advanced battery technologies that use less or no lithium, such as solid-state batteries, to reduce the environmental and geopolitical risks associated with lithium mining and supply chain.

Ukraine’s top guns need new jets to win the war

23 April 2023

Ukraine's air force has lost 60 fighter jets since Russia invaded Ukraine last year, and the country's air balance is looking shaky. Ukraine needs a fresh fleet of fighter jets, and the Gripen, built by Saab, is one candidate. However, the main problem with the Gripen is that Sweden has sold or leased around 66 countries, and there are fewer than 100 available. Ukraine is also eying the second-hand market, but the cost of resurfacing airfields would invite Russian missiles. The current situation in Ukraine is similar, with the US and its allies providing military aid to Ukraine, while Russia provides military aid to separatist rebels in eastern Ukraine. The debate over fighter jets is just one aspect of this larger conflict.

19 April 2023

Wars are lasting longer due to growing complexity, criminality, and climate change. Peacemaking is hard but necessary, and it often involves starting unofficial talks, including women and civil-society groups in the peace process, and experimenting with different peacemaking templates.

Opinion: The increasing duration of civil wars is a global calamity that requires urgent attention. Historical events, such as the Arab Spring, have contributed to the rise in battle deaths and the total death toll. Policies that promote peace and build better institutions in fragile states are necessary to curb the effects of climate change and prevent conflicts. The president should consider investing in these policies to prevent further bloodshed and promote global peace.
